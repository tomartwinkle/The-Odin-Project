Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert known for its rich, creamy layers and
distinct coffee flavor. The name "tiramisu" translates to "pick me up," referring to the invigorating combination
of coffee and cocoa.

Ingredients

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese (room temperature)
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream (chilled)
  • 2 cups strong brewed coffee or espresso (cooled)
  • 1/4 cup coffee liqueur (optional, such as Kahl√∫a)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 packs ladyfingers (savoiardi biscuits)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional, for garnish)

Directions

  1. Prepare the mascarpone mixture:
    In a heatproof bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar.
    Place the bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler method) and whisk continuously until the mixture becomes thick
    and pale in color (about 5-8 minutes). Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
    Once cooled, fold in the mascarpone cheese until smooth and creamy.
  2. Whip the cream:
    In a separate bowl, whisk the heavy cream and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form. Be careful not to over-whip it.
  3. Combine the mascarpone and whipped cream:
    Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until combined and smooth. Set aside.
  4. Prepare the coffee dip:
    In a shallow dish, combine the cooled coffee (or espresso) with coffee liqueur (if using).
  5. Assemble the tiramisu:
    Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture, ensuring both sides are soaked but not too soggy (just a couple of seconds).
    Place them in a single layer in the bottom of your serving dish (9x9 inch or similar).
    Spread half of the mascarpone cream mixture over the layer of ladyfingers.
    Add another layer of coffee-soaked ladyfingers on top of the cream.
    Spread the remaining mascarpone mixture over the second layer of ladyfingers, smoothing it evenly.
  6. Chill the tiramisu:
    Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. This allows the flavors to meld
    and the dessert to set properly.
  7. Serve:
    Before serving, dust the top generously with unsweetened cocoa powder.
    Optionally, garnish with dark chocolate shavings.
