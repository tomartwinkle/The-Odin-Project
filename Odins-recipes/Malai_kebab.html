Malai Kebab

Description

Malai kebab is a popular Indian dish known for its tender and juicy texture, typically made from marinated pieces of chicken
or paneer (Indian cottage cheese). The name "malai" refers to cream, which is a key ingredient in the marinade that gives
the kebabs their rich and creamy flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • 1/4 cup thick yogurt (hung curd or Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons cashew paste (soaked cashews ground into a paste)
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper powder (or black pepper powder)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon butter or ghee (melted, for basting)
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped coriander (for garnish)

Directions

  1. Marinate the chicken:
    In a large bowl, combine the cream, yogurt, cashew paste, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, pepper, lemon juice, garam masala,
    and salt. Mix well. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and coat them evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours
    (or overnight for best results).
  2. Skewer the chicken:
    Once the chicken has marinated, thread the pieces onto skewers. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes
    to prevent burning.
  3. Preheat the grill/oven:
    Preheat your grill or oven to 200°C (390°F). If using an oven, you can also use the broil function for a charred effect.
  4. Grill the kebabs: Place the skewers on the grill or in the oven. Baste the chicken with melted butter or ghee. Grill for 12-15 minutes,
    turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred at the edges. Baste with butter or
    ghee a couple of times during grilling for added flavor and moisture.
  5. Serve:
    Remove the kebabs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped coriander.
    Serve hot with mint chutney, onion rings, and lemon wedges.
