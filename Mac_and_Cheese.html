Mac and Cheese

Description

Mac and cheese, short for macaroni and cheese, is a classic comfort food dish made primarily of cooked
macaroni pasta and a creamy cheese sauce. The dish typically features a blend of cheeses, such as cheddar
Gruyère, or mozzarella, combined with a béchamel sauce (a white sauce made from butter, flour, and milk)
or a simpler cheese sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups elbow macaroni (or any other pasta you prefer)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk (whole milk or 2%)
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese (sharp cheddar for more flavor)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard powder (optional, for flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika (for garnish)
  • Breadcrumbs (optional, for topping)

Directions

  1. Cook the pasta:
    Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the macaroni and cook according to package instructions until
    al dente (firm to the bite). Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the cheese sauce:
    In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the flour and whisk continuously
    for about 1-2 minutes to create a roux (a thick paste). This helps thicken the sauce.
  3. Add the cheese: Once the sauce is thickened, reduce the heat to low. Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese and Parmesan cheese
    until melted and smooth. Add mustard powder, garlic powder (if using), salt, and pepper.
    Stir until everything is well combined and the sauce is creamy.
  4. Combine pasta and sauce:
    Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir until the pasta is well coated.
  5. Optional baking step:
    If you like a crispy topping, preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Transfer the mac and cheese to a baking dish.
    Sprinkle breadcrumbs and a little extra cheese on top. Bake for about 15-20 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  6. Serve:
    Garnish with a sprinkle of paprika and serve hot.
